Geometric Duality

Alex Becht, University of Denver

This applet, written in HTML5 and ES6 JavaScript, offers a visualization tool for the principles and key elements of geometric duality, a useful technique for solving computational geometry problems. For a discussion on what this principle exactly entails, follow this link. The general rule, however, is that a point (a,b) in the primal plane corresponds to the line y=ax—b in the dual plane, and vice versa.

Instructions

Insertion

To add a new point in either the primal or the dual plane, simply click anywhere on the coordinate planes while the insert mode is selected. To create a line segment, click and drag, releasing where you want the endpoint to be. The dual of these operations will be automatically rendered in the other plane. To draw a line, create a corresponding point in the other plane and use the resulting line.

Editing

To move points or segments, select the move mode, then click on a point and move it anywhere in the canvas. To delete points, select the delete mode and then click on any point.
